Terrasmart / Warranty Claims Expenditure
DASHBOARD DATE: APRIL 8, 2026  ·  DATA: 2025 FULL YEAR vs 2026 YTD
As of April 8, 2026, Terrasmart has spent $415,762 on warranty claims in 2026 YTD across 190 claims (169 approved, 21 denied), compared to $7,535,164 for the full year 2025 across 411 claims. TerraTrak-2P is the dominant cost driver in both years, representing 78% of 2026 spend. The 2026 YTD figure is expected to be substantially lower than 2025 full-year totals — year-over-year comparisons should be interpreted with caution given that 2026 data covers only a partial year (Q1 + early Q2).

⚙ Calculation Methodology — Executive Summary +

Spend categorization: "Parts Only" claims = material component replacements shipped to site, no labor billed — equivalent to material-only/lower-liability exposure. "Site Visit" claims = field service dispatched, includes both material and labor costs — equivalent to self-performed/higher-liability exposure.

2025 cost breakdown: Total $7.54M = $727K parts-only material + $924K site-visit material + $5.88M site-visit labor. Labor-heavy warranty work drove the majority of 2025 spend. TerraTrak-2P alone accounted for $4.17M (55%).

2026 cost breakdown: Total $415K = $134K parts-only + $91K site-visit material + $191K site-visit labor. The labor component (46% of total) is much lower than 2025 (78% of total), suggesting a different claim mix — more component replacements, fewer large field service dispatches in 2026 YTD.

Product line color coding: Colors are consistent across all charts. TerraTrak-2P is highlighted as the dominant cost driver in both years.
